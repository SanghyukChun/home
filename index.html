Description

I'm currently a machine learning researcher in Clova AI Research (CLAIR). My research focuses on understanding leaned representations by machine learning models. Especially, I'm now interested in the robustness and the uncertainty estimation of deep models. Although my current works are mainly focused on the computer vision, I have broad interests in general data domains, including music information retrieval, speech enhancement, natural language processing, and recommender systems.

One of my strong points is extensive experiences both in academic and industry; I worked as a software engineer in IUM-SOCIUS (web developer) and Kakao (software developer, research engineer) three years. Also, I have experienced in various research areas: Robustness and Uncertainty Classification Generative Models Audio Processing Recommender Systems Real-World Systems

Biography

I received a Master's degree in Electrical Engineering from KAIST (advised by Prof Jinwoo Shin). During the Master's degree, I researched on a scalable algorithm for robust subspace clustering.

I did an internship at IUM-SOCIUS in 2012 as a software developer (keywords: JAVA spring, JAVA spring batch, Python Django, Ruby on Rails, MongoDB). During summer 2013, I did a research internship at Networked and Distributed Computing System Lab in KAIST (keywords: real-time and large-scale network system, real-time and large-scale indexing system). Also, I had a research internship experience in Naver Labs during the fall 2015 (keywords: deep model normalization).

After I received a Master's degree, I worked as a research engineer at advanced recommendation team (ART) in Kakao from 2016 to 2018. In Kakao, I covered software engineering for a large-scale real-time recommender system (Toros), content-based representation modeling (for textual, visual, and musical data), collaborative filtering modeling, user embedding, user clustering, and ranking system based on Multi-armed Bandit. Finally, I joined Clova AI Research (CLAIR) in 2018.
